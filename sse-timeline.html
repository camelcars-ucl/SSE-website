Software system Engineering over the years!

1945-1965

The Origins Of software

The Era in which the first programming language was being created to solve general problems. However, the term 'software' was not in use in this span.

1965-1985

The NATO conferences and the crisis

NATO held two conferences leading to the beginning of Software engineering and the word 'software' was also first introduced in this conference. It was not long before the SSE was hit by software crisis- defined by quality of software- which was for over 20 years. Below are the factors affected.

  • Cost and Budget Overruns
  • Property Damage( Usually stealth of identities)
  • Life and Death(Example is Therac-25

Moreover, the list of the software problems has been kept by Peter G. Neumann.

1985-1989

Overcoming the crisis!

For companies producing the software it was primary for them to solve the crisis but the solutions proposed were all labelled as 'Silver Bullets'. Shortly, after there were debates and finally the 'No Silver Bullets' article was published stating that no solutions will be ever developed. To approach the problem Agile methodology was being developed.

1990-1999

The Era of Internet

The internet was growing rapidly due to the demand for email systems. Since the programs were required to handle a large number of media including the optimistion of images. In relation to SSE, the design of the Email became a issue and required pre-screening.Therefore, a simpler approach was needed to overcome this issue.

2000-2007

Methodlogies

To reduce the cost of producing the software by different organizations, led to the growth of easy and faster methodologies making it easier to develop better quality software quickly. The Agile methodology evolved by introducing new aspects such as pair programming. The SWEBOK has been tablled as an ISO standard.

2010-

Stuxnet Virus

The Stuxnet virus was found by Kaspersky- an antivirus organization. The virus was used to damage Iran's nuclear program- the damage done remained unknown. It is believed by experts that Stuxnet required largest effort in malware history by highly skilled programmers interested in attacking infrastructure such as Iran's Uranium enrichment facility.

2014

Heartbleed Bug Discovered

Heartbleed was uncovered in 2014 and it was given its name bacuase of an extension in OpenSSL called 'Heartbeat' and since, it was leaking sensitive information of around 66% of the internet the name 'Heartbleed was given'. OpenSSL is a software library that is used by applications for secure communication which had a major security flaw in the code. There was also a attach on Sony in which the hackers got hold of more than a hundred of terabytes of sensitive data.
